The Ritual

01

Prepare Your Glass

Place a rocks glass (also called an Old Fashioned glass) in the freezer for at least 15 minutes. A chilled glass keeps your Negroni perfectly cold without over-dilution.

02

Add the Ice

Fill your chilled glass with a large ice cube or sphere. The larger the ice, the slower it melts ‚Äî this is crucial for a Negroni that evolves beautifully as you sip.

03

The Equal Measure

Pour equal parts (1 oz each) of Gin, Campari, and Sweet Vermouth directly into the glass. Use a jigger for precision ‚Äî balance is everything in a Negroni.

04

The Gentle Stir

Using a bar spoon, stir gently for 30-45 seconds in a circular motion. Unlike shaking, stirring preserves the silky texture and prevents over-dilution. The goal is to chill and marry the flavors, not to shake them apart.

05

The Orange Peel Garnish

Using a sharp peeler or knife, cut a wide strip of orange peel. Hold it over the glass, colored side down, and gently squeeze to express the oils onto the surface. Then, run the peel around the rim and drop it into the glass.

‚ú¶ Pro Tips from the Masters ‚ú¶

  • Invest in good ice: Clear, large ice cubes from directional freezing look stunning and melt slowly. Make them in advance.
  • Store vermouth in the fridge: Unlike spirits, vermouth is wine-based and oxidizes. Keep it refrigerated and use within 2-3 months of opening.
  • Try the "Build in Glass" method: For a more casual approach, simply pour all ingredients over ice and stir. No shaker needed.
  • Experiment with gin: A floral gin (like Hendrick's) creates a different Negroni than a classic London Dry. Both are delicious.
  • The "Negroni Sbagliato" variation: Replace gin with Prosecco for a lighter, bubbly version ‚Äî "sbagliato" means "mistaken" in Italian.
  • Take your time: The Negroni is not a shot. It's an experience. Pour, sit, sip, and appreciate the craftsmanship of over 100 years of cocktail history.
